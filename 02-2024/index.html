Black History Month at the Library

To celebrate Black History Month, we're highlighting two displays. The first is a new digital collection curated by the Guelph Black Students Association and the University of Guelph's Cultural Diversity team in Student Experience, titled Black History Month 2024: 360°; Health and Wellness. The collection focuses on a holistic approach to health and wellness and showcases works by historically underrepresented Black voices to foster inclusivity, education, empowerment, and community building.

And, if you're in the library, check out the Black History Month Gryph Reads display, and browse a variety of works by Black authors

Library News

  • Winter Pride 2024 runs from Feb. 9 to Feb. 19 and to celebrate, we're highlighting a new digital collection curated by OUTline. The collection includes works on a variety of topics ranging from fiction with queer characters to historical accounts documenting queer advancement, and invites readers to explore, reflect, and celebrate the LGBTQ2IA+ community. Explore the Winter Pride 2024 digital collection.

  • The library’s undergraduate survey is now open! If you’re a student at U of G, we want to hear from you. Share your experience to help us improve library study spaces and services. Upon completion of the survey, you can enter a draw to win one of several Hospitality Services gift cards. Start the survey now.

  • Love Data Week, an international celebration, is happening from Feb. 12 to 16. To celebrate, the library wants to share how we can support you in navigating your way through the research data lifecycle. Whether you’re a student or a researcher, we have resources available for you. Learn more about our data services and explore Love Data Week events.

English Language Speaking Series

The English Language Speaking Series is on now at the library! This six-week series takes place every Tuesday until mid-March. If you’re an undergraduate student, graduate student, postdoctoral scholar, or visiting scholar who uses English as an Additional Language, this series is for you. Each week features a different focus and you’re welcome to attend just one session or all of them. Upcoming topics include:

  • Building Presentation Skills
  • Communicating with Professors and Supervisors
  • Participating in Discussion
  • Communicating as a TA
  • Practicing Workplace Speaking Strategies

Learn more and register for the English Language Speaking Series.
