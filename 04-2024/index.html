April 2024

Exam Stress Busters at the Library

Exams are coming up, and as such, so are exam stress busters! If you're visiting the library to study, we encourage you to take a break and enjoy some of the activities we've prepared for you. Post a positive message for your peers on the Positive Wall, drop a worry in the Worry Box, or pick up an Activity Book or page to help you take a break from studying. Balance matters! Learn more about the events happening at the library for Exam Stress Busters, as well as links to helpful studying resources.

Calling All Foodies - New Exhibit on Now!

Interested in learning more about the Canadian food scene and how it's changed overtime? If so, we encourage you to check out Cooking Up History: 30 Years of the Culinary Historians of Canada (CHC), the exhibit that just launched in our Archival & Special Collections Exhibit Room. This exhibit not only commemorates the 30th anniversary of the CHC—it also highlights the inductees of Taste Canada's Hall of Fame. Learn more about this student-curated exhibit and how you can browse it over the next number of months.

Preparing for Exams? You Need This Guide

The library isn't just a place to study (though it is a great space to do so). It's also a place that provides exceptional support and services, thanks to our great staff and peer helpers. That said, we know exam time is upon us, and so we want to share with you the Succeed at Exams online guide. In it, you'll find info about:

  • Setting up your study space
  • Managing exam stress
  • Multiple choice exams
  • Essay exams
  • Problem-solving exams
  • Short-answer exams
  • And, more!

Get to Know the Library Peer Helpers

At the library, we're lucky to have incredible students who work with us in a variety of roles through the peer helper program. Working on a variety of library teams, peer helpers provide student-to-student support to enhance the academic journey for students at U of G. Want to meet some of our peer helpers? Visit the library's Instagram page to read some of their bios.

New Gryph Reads Display - April Showers

We feel a variety of feelings each day. This month, our Gryph Reads display features works that bring forward strong emotions, like sadness, with the hope it allows a release to make room for new growth with spring upon us. Browse the display in-person on the first floor of the library. Some titles featured include:

  • Perks of Being a Wallflower by Shephen Chbosky
  • My Sister’s Keeper by Jodi Picoult
  • Crying in H Mart by Michelle Zauner
